Jeddah to Makkah Route Overview

The journey from Jeddah to Makkah is one of the most traveled routes in Saudi Arabia, carrying millions of pilgrims each year to perform Umrah and Hajj. The 100-kilometer route connects the Red Sea port city of Jeddah with the holy city of Makkah, traversing diverse landscapes from coastal plains to mountainous terrain.

Route Details

  • Primary Route: Highway 40 (Makkah Road) via Hudaithi Road
  • Distance: Approximately 100 kilometers (62 miles)
  • Typical Duration: 60-90 minutes under normal traffic
  • Peak Travel Times: Can extend to 2+ hours during rush hour and Hajj season
  • Road Quality: Excellent multi-lane highway with clear signage
  • Toll Charges: SAR 5-10 depending on route

Best Times to Travel

  • Recommended: Early morning (5:00-7:00 AM) or late evening (9:00-11:00 PM)
  • Avoid: Friday mornings (Jumu'ah prayers), rush hours (7-9 AM, 5-7 PM)
  • Ramadan: Iftar time (sunset) and late night can be busy
  • Hajj Season: Book well in advance, allow extra time

About Jeddah and Makkah

Jeddah - The Gateway to Makkah

Jeddah is Saudi Arabia's commercial capital and the main gateway for pilgrims traveling to Makkah. Located on the Red Sea coast, it's home to King Abdulaziz International Airport, the main entry point for Umrah and Hajj pilgrims.

Key Facts:

  • Population: 4.5+ million
  • Airport: King Abdulaziz International (JED)
  • Attractions: Al-Balad (UNESCO site), Red Sea coast, Corniche
  • Climate: Hot, humid coastal climate
  • Significance: Commercial hub, pilgrimage gateway

Popular Pickup Locations:

  • King Abdulaziz International Airport (all terminals)
  • Al-Balad (Old Town)
  • Jeddah Corniche
  • City center hotels
  • Business districts
  • Red Sea Mall area

Makkah - The Holiest City

Makkah is the holiest city in Islam, home to Masjid al-Haram and the Kaaba. Millions of Muslims visit annually to perform Umrah and Hajj pilgrimages. The city has modern infrastructure to accommodate pilgrims while maintaining its spiritual atmosphere.

Key Facts:

  • Population: 2.5 million (15+ million during Hajj)
  • Elevation: 277 meters above sea level
  • Attractions: Masjid al-Haram, Kaaba, Jabal al-Nour
  • Climate: Hot desert, cooler than Jeddah
  • Significance: Birthplace of Prophet Muhammad, holiest Islamic site

Hotel Districts:

  • Clock Tower: Luxury hotels adjacent to Haram
  • Aziziyah: Budget-friendly, 5-8km from Haram
  • Al Kakiyyah: Mid-range apartments
  • Al Nuzha: Residential, quieter area
