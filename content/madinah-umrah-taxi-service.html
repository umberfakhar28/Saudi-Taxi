Rawdah Sharif Access Support

The Rawdah (Riyad al-Jannah) is the area between the Prophet's pulpit (minbar) and his house, considered a garden from the gardens of Paradise. Access requires the Nusuk app reservation.

How We Can Help:

  • Transportation to the Prophet's Mosque for your scheduled Rawdah time
  • Information about the Nusuk app registration process
  • Guidance to the correct entrance gates
  • Elderly and wheelchair assistance
  • Early morning transport (recommended time)

Tips for Rawdah Visit:

  • Book your slot through Nusuk app in advance
  • Early morning slots (after Fajr) are less crowded
  • Dress modestly and be in state of wudu
  • Follow mosque etiquette and staff instructions
  • Women have designated times

Frequently Asked Questions

How far is Madinah Airport from Prophet's Mosque?

Prince Mohammad bin Abdulaziz Airport (MED) is approximately 15 kilometers from Masjid an-Nabawi (Prophet's Mosque). The journey takes 20-30 minutes by taxi.

How do I book Rawdah access?

Rawdah access is managed through the Nusuk app (formerly Eatmarna). Download the app, register, and book your preferred time slot. We provide transportation to the mosque for your scheduled time.

What are the best times to visit Prophet's Mosque?

The mosque is beautiful at all times, but many prefer:

  • Early morning: After Fajr (dawn) for peaceful atmosphere
  • Evening: Before Maghrib for cooler temperatures
  • Late night: Tahajjud time for spiritual reflection

Can women visit all Ziyarat sites?

Yes, all Ziyarat sites in Madinah are accessible to women. The Rawdah has designated times for women. Mount Uhud, Quba Mosque, and all other sites welcome female visitors.

How long should I plan to stay in Madinah?

Most Umrah pilgrims stay 2-4 days in Madinah. This allows time for:

  • Daily prayers at Prophet's Mosque
  • Rawdah visit
  • Ziyarat tour (half-day or full-day)
  • Rest and reflection
