Significance: Site of Hira Cave where Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) received the first revelation of the Quran.

  • Distance from Haram: 4 kilometers
  • Travel time: 15-20 minutes
  • Hiking time: 45-90 minutes (1,200+ steps)

Our service takes you to the base of the mountain. The climb requires good physical fitness. We recommend comfortable walking shoes and bringing water. Elderly pilgrims or those with mobility issues may prefer to view from the base.

Jabal Thawr (Mount Thawr)

Significance: Location of the cave where Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) and Abu Bakr hid during the Hijrah to Madinah.

  • Distance from Haram: 12 kilometers south
  • Travel time: 25-30 minutes
  • Accessibility: Vehicle reaches closer than Jabal al-Nour
  • Best time: Morning or evening

Less strenuous than Jabal al-Nour, this site offers easier access. The cave is visible from the road, making it suitable for all pilgrims including elderly and those with limited mobility.

Yes, but advance booking is essential during Hajj (Dhul-Hijjah). We recommend booking at least 2-4 weeks ahead. About Makkah - The Holiest City in Islam

Historical Significance

Makkah (Mecca) is the holiest city in Islam and the birthplace of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). It is home to the Kaaba, the most sacred site in Islam, located within Masjid al-Haram (the Grand Mosque).

Every year, millions of Muslims perform Hajj and Umrah pilgrimages to Makkah, fulfilling one of the Five Pillars of Islam. The city's significance dates back thousands of years to the time of Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham) who built the Kaaba with his son Ismail.

Modern Makkah

Today, Makkah is a modern city with world-class infrastructure designed to accommodate over 15 million visitors during Hajj season. The Abraj Al Bait complex features the world's third-tallest building and largest clock tower, alongside luxury hotels overlooking the Haram.

The city has undergone massive development including expanded mosque capacity (now accommodating 4 million worshippers), high-speed rail connections, and modern transportation networks.

Key Information for Pilgrims

  • Population: 2.5 million (15+ million during Hajj)
  • Elevation: 277 meters above sea level
  • Climate: Hot desert climate
  • Best time to visit: November-March (cooler months)
  • Entry restrictions: Muslims only in central areas
  • Currency: Saudi Riyal (SAR)
  • Language: Arabic (English widely understood in tourist areas)

Major Attractions in Makkah

  • Masjid al-Haram (Grand Mosque) - The holiest mosque containing the Kaaba
  • Kaaba - The cubic structure toward which Muslims pray
  • Abraj Al Bait - Clock Tower complex with hotels and shopping
  • Jabal al-Nour - Mountain with Hira Cave
  • Jabal Thawr - Mountain with Thawr Cave
  • Jannat al-Mu'alla - Historic cemetery
  • Makkah Museum - Islamic history and artifacts
